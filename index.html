1.0 Gang Code of Conduct

1.1 Dress Code ‚Äî Gang Representation

  • Minimum of three identifiable uniform pieces must be worn during gang-related RP.
  • Uniforms must match gang colors and identity.
  • Wearing gang attire outside of gang RP is discouraged.

1.2 Vehicle Guidelines ‚Äî Gang Representation

  • All members must use the same vehicle color scheme.
  • Vehicles should match gang identity.
  • Changing colors mid-operation to avoid identification is prohibited.
  • Police, government, or emergency-style vehicles require staff approval.

1.3 Toxicity & Conduct

  • Talking over bodies is allowed.
  • Toxicity must be kept to a minimum and remain within city/community rules.
  • Excessive harassment, hate speech, real-life threats, or targeted abuse are strictly prohibited.
  • Staff may intervene if behavior is deemed excessive or disruptive.

1.4 Org Rebranding ‚Äî Identity Changes

  • Any changes to a gang‚Äôs name, colors, theme, or branding must be approved.

2.0 Gang Hostilities & Conflict Rules

2.1 War Declaration Requirements

  • All war declarations must be approved by Org Staff.
  • Wars must be submitted through the DEC Channel using the official template (see pinned messages).
  • No war is valid without staff approval.
  • Spawn camping is prohibited.
  • Holding compounds or territory without proper RP escalation is not allowed.

2.2 Hand Holding ‚Äî Unrealistic Cooperation

  • Hand holding of any kind is prohibited.
  • Gangs may not share turf, jointly defend compounds, or operate as a single organization.
  • External gangs must not interfere in active hostilities unless they have an active declaration against the same organization.
  • Temporary cooperation must have clear RP reasoning and may require staff approval.

2.3 Escalation Rules ‚Äî Conflict Progression

  • Hostilities must build naturally through roleplay.
  • Meetings or verbal confrontations.
  • Melee fights, kidnappings, or interrogations.
  • Official War Declarations after RP buildup.

Escalation should reflect a naturally developing conflict. Organizations are expected to allow hostilities to build through continued roleplay before initiating KOS, which may ultimately progress into an official war declaration.

3.0 Gang Structure & Limits

3.1 Member & Prospect Requirements

Prospects are additional and do not count toward full member totals.

Tier Full Member Requirement (Minimum) Prospect Limit (Additional)
Trial Crew Minimum 5 Members +2 Prospects
Crew Minimum 6 Members +2 Prospects
Organization Minimum 14 Members +4 Prospects
Syndicate Minimum 20 Members +4 Prospects

3.2 Structure Guidelines

  • Organizations must maintain their minimum member requirement to retain their tier.
  • Falling below minimum requirements for an extended period may result in a status review.
  • Prospect slots are separate from full member limits.
  • Exceeding prospect limits without staff approval is not permitted.
  • Prospects are considered trial members and should not hold leadership or decision-making roles.
  • Prospects are not permitted to participate in gang warfare or organized hostile actions.
  • If a hostile situation begins between organizations (Crew, Organization, or Syndicate), prospects must immediately disengage and leave the area.
  • Tier changes must be approved through an Org Management Ticket.

3.3 Gang Responsibility & Integrity

  • Organizations are responsible for the actions of their members.
  • Cheating, exploiting, third-party software, glitches, or knowingly benefiting from cheats may result in strikes or disbandment.
  • Consequences may include warnings/strikes, financial penalties, hostility cooldowns, suspension, or forced disbandment.

4.0 Enforcement & Final Notes

  • Individual behavior reflects on the entire organization.
  • Repeated violations will result in escalating penalties.
  • Staff decisions are final.
